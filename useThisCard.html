Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 18, 2026  ·  Use This Card

Use This Card ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have. By using the app you agree to the practices described here.

1. Information We Collect

Stored only on your device
DataPurpose
Your saved credit cards Identify which cards are in your wallet so we can recommend the best one for each purchase category.
Widget category preferences Determine which spending categories appear on your home screen widget.
Onboarding completion flag Skip the welcome screens on subsequent launches.
Collected by third-party services
DataServicePurpose
Anonymous usage events Firebase Analytics (Google) Understand which features are used so we can improve the app.
Credit card catalog Cloud Firestore (Google) Fetch the latest card reward rates and benefits. No personal data is sent.
Crash & diagnostic data Firebase (Google) Detect and fix technical issues.

We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, financial account details, or any other personally identifiable information. The credit cards you add to your wallet are stored exclusively on your device using iOS's secure local storage.

2. Device Sensors

The app uses your device's accelerometer to create interactive tilt effects on card visuals. Sensor readings are processed entirely on-device in real time and are never stored or transmitted.

3. Home Screen Widget

The home screen widget reads your saved cards and category preferences from shared local storage (App Groups) to display your best card recommendation without opening the app. This data never leaves your device.

4. Background Refresh

The app periodically runs a background task (at most once per hour, subject to iOS scheduling) to fetch the latest card catalog from Firestore and refresh your widget. Only the card catalog — no personal data — is downloaded during this process.

5. App Tracking Transparency

On iOS 14.5 and later, we ask for permission to track your activity across other apps and websites using Apple's App Tracking Transparency framework. If you allow tracking, Firebase Analytics may use your device's advertising identifier (IDFA) to provide aggregated, anonymised analytics. If you decline, analytics continues without the IDFA. You can change your choice at any time in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.

6. Third-Party Services

  • Google Firebase — Analytics, Firestore, and performance monitoring. Governed by Google's Privacy Policy .

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your information to any other third parties.

7. Data Retention

Data stored on your device remains until you delete the app or clear app data in iOS Settings. Anonymous analytics data retained by Firebase follows Google's standard retention schedule (up to 14 months by default).

8. Children's Privacy

Use This Card is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will promptly delete it.

9. Your Rights

  • Access & deletion — All personal preferences are stored on your device. Delete the app to remove all local data instantly.
  • Opt out of analytics — Disable analytics in Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking or by using Apple's "Limit Ad Tracking" option.
  • CCPA / GDPR — Because we do not collect personally identifiable information, most data subject rights (access, portability, erasure) are satisfied by deleting the app.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated via an in-app notice or an App Store update note. Continued use of the app after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.
